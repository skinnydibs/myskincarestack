## Design Context

### Users
Lynn + a small circle of friends. Personal daily use — checking a routine, adding products, referencing ingredient combos. Used on mobile (iPhone) and occasionally desktop. The sharing angle means it needs to feel polished and intentional, not like a dev prototype.

### Brand Personality
Personal, luxe, warm. Feels like a curated beauty brand meets personal journal. Not clinical, not techy. Think Glow Recipe meets a well-designed iOS app — approachable but elevated.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Reference**: Glowup Dribbble design (https://cdn.dribbble.com/userupload/47227377/file/fbfc8a9abf710385ef04ee5bed2df1ba.png) — warm cream/off-white base, soft blush pink accents, large border-radius everywhere, clean humanist sans-serif typography, generous whitespace, very soft shadows (not harsh drop shadows). Cards feel light and airy. No dark mode. No clinical blues or harsh contrast.

**Palette direction**: Stay warm — cream, beige, blush, terracotta. The current orange-coral accent (#F07C3A) fits but could be softened. Avoid cool greys, pure black, neon, or anything that reads "tech startup."

**Anti-references**: Clinical/medical UI, dark mode with glowing accents, heavy card grids, anything that feels like a dashboard or developer tool.

### Design Principles
1. **Warm over clinical** — Every element should feel personal and human, not like a data app
2. **Breathe** — Generous whitespace. Resist filling space. Luxury is about what you leave out.
3. **Soft luxury** — Large radii, gentle shadows, warm neutrals. Never harsh borders or flat corporate styling.
4. **Mobile-first** — Primary user is on iPhone. Touch targets, thumb zones, and readability on small screens come first.
5. **Curated, not overwhelming** — Progressive disclosure. Show the essentials; let users drill down. Don't show everything at once.
